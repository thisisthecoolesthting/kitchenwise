Lodge 10.25” Cast Iron Skillet — The All-Rounder That Belongs in Every Kitchen

A 10.25” cast iron skillet handles a 4-serving breakfast, 2 chicken thighs with a crust, a cornbread loaf, and a shallow braise without the pan ever becoming the limiting factor. At $34, the Lodge is the reference recommendation at this price.

Heat distribution vs. stainless

Cast iron heats slowly and unevenly compared to stainless with aluminum cores. The handle end of a cold Lodge skillet lags the center by 40°F for the first 5 minutes of preheating. The fix: preheat on low for 5 minutes before raising heat. Once up to temperature, cast iron’s thermal mass holds heat through protein searing better than lighter pans.

Seasoning maintenance

Lodge ships pre-seasoned with vegetable oil. The surface improves with use. After the first 6 months of cooking — searing, roasting, stovetop — the surface reaches near-nonstick performance for eggs. Avoid dish soap for daily washing; hot water and a chain mail scrubber is standard practice.

Oven compatibility

Safe to 500°F. This makes it the best pan in most kitchens for finishing a sear-then-roast cook, making skillet cookies, and cornbread in a preheated oven.
