# GSI Pinnacle Camper Cookset — Group Cooking Without the Weight Penalty

The GSI Pinnacle Camper packs a 3.2L pot, 2L pot, 7" frying pan, cutting board lid, four nested
bowls, four mugs, four sporks, and a strainer into a 2 lb 8 oz nested system. For a group of
3–4 on a 3-night car camping or canoe trip, this replaces a full kitchen kit.

## Non-stick performance

The Teflon-coated pots cook eggs and oatmeal without the burned-on residue typical of uncoated
aluminum. At 2 years of quarterly use, the coating on our test set shows light scratching but no
delamination. Use only plastic or silicone utensils — metal utensils destroy Teflon quickly.

## Heat efficiency

The GSI uses hard-anodized aluminum, which heats faster than stainless and more evenly than
uncoated aluminum. On a 10,000 BTU canister stove, 1 liter of 65°F water reached boiling
in 4 minutes 20 seconds — within 10% of the Jetboil's 4-minute performance.

## Vs. Jetboil

For a group of 4, the Pinnacle makes more sense: larger volume, a frying pan, and nesting
bowls replace individual systems. Jetboil wins for speed and fuel efficiency for 1–2 people.
