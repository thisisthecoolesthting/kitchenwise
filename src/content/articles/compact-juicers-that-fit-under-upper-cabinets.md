Compact juicers that fit under upper cabinets are a game changer for anyone with limited kitchen space. ## Why compact juicers make sense for small kitchens

When you live with cramped counters or under cabinets just above your food prep area, bulky juicers are more hassle than help. My kitchen space is limited, and bulky juicers have either forced me to store them away or move them around constantly. That's why I focused on juicers specifically designed or sized to fit under standard kitchen cabinetry, which is often about 18 inches tall from countertop to bottom of the upper cabinets.

Compact juicers free up valuable real estate, but they need to maintain good performance. Many small juicers I’ve tested sacrifice power or clog quickly with fibrous ingredients. The best ones strike a balance — maintaining enough motor strength and design innovation to extract juice efficiently without dominating your counter.

## What makes a juicer fit under upper cabinets?

The critical dimension here is height. Average upper cabinets hang about 18 inches above a counter, though measuring your own is always wise. You want a juicer’s tallest point to be well under this limit, ideally 14–16 inches, to give room for attachments like pulp containers or lids. Width and depth also matter for overall footprint but typically height is the biggest barrier.

Besides size, consider:
- **Low-profile design:** Vertical masticating juicers tend to be taller, whereas horizontal models often work better in tight spaces.
- **Detachable parts:** Units that break down into smaller pieces are easier to store if you don’t keep them permanently on the counter.
- **User-friendly features:** Wide chutes minimize prep time, which is helpful when workflow is constrained.
- **Quiet operation:** Small kitchens echo, so look for quieter motors.

## Centrifugal vs. masticating juicers for small kitchens

| Factor                  | Centrifugal Juicers                | Masticating Juicers                    |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Height and size         | Generally shorter, more compact  | Often taller, but some models fit under cabinets |
| Juice speed            | Faster (up to 60+ RPM)            | Slower (40–80 RPM)                    |
| Juice quality          | More foam, less nutrient retention | Higher nutrient retention, less oxidation |
| Noise level            | Louder due to fast spinning       | Quieter operation                     |
| Cleaning complexity    | More parts, tricky fine mesh      | Usually simpler, less fine mesh       |
| Versatility            | Primarily fruits and hard veggies | Also good for leafy greens, nut butters |

In my experience, centrifugal juicers like the Breville Compact Juice Fountain offer speed and low height, great for quick mornings. Masticating juicers take longer but yield better, longer-lasting juice; choosing a compact model like Hurom’s H101 is ideal if you prioritize juice quality and have a bit more patience.

## How to measure your space for a compact juicer

Before buying, measure the height from your countertop to the bottom of the lowest upper cabinet. Standard is about 18 inches, but kitchens vary. Don’t forget to:
- Leave 1-2 inches of clearance above and behind for plugs and ventilation.
- Check the depth and width if your counter space is tight.
- Consider removing accessories that add height like pulp containers or lids during storage if you store the unit under cabinets but assemble when in use.

I keep a foldable tape measure handy for quick checks, and measure twice to avoid surprises.

## Cleaning and maintenance tips for compact juicers

Cleaning is a pain point for many juicers and extra so if your kitchen is small with no dedicated cleanup area:

- **Look for dishwasher-safe parts:** I always prefer models like Hurom H101 or Omega J8006HDS with components that tolerate dishwasher cycles.
- **Rinse immediately:** Juice residue dries fast, making parts harder to clean.
- **Use included brushes:** Many compact juicers come with specifically designed brushes to get pulp out of mesh filters quickly.
- **Disassemble completely:** Smaller parts clean faster and reduce mold build-up if dried properly.
- **Consider a juice cleaning cycle:** Some models have features that flush out pulp automatically.

These little habits keep your juicer ready to go, especially when space is at a premium.

## Final thoughts on choosing a small-footprint juicer

It’s possible to enjoy fresh-pressed juice daily without sacrificing valuable counter or shelf space with the right compact juicer. Identifying your kitchen’s exact space limits and weighing your juice preferences — speed vs nutritional quality — will guide your decision.

Personally, I lean toward vertical masticating juicers that fit under my cabinets because I want max nutrition and low noise, even if juicing takes a few minutes more. But if you’re an early riser who needs juice fast, a low-profile centrifugal juicer like the Breville Compact Juice Fountain Plus might be your best bet.

Trying out a few models if you can, especially in-store for size, or prioritizing brands with excellent customer support, really helps avoid disappointment.

## FAQ

### What is the average height clearance under upper cabinets?

Most upper cabinets are about 18 inches above the countertop. Measuring your kitchen ensures your chosen juicer fits comfortably with space for electrical cords and ventilation.

### Are centrifugal or masticating juicers better for tight spaces?

Centrifugal juicers are typically shorter and faster, making them well-suited for tighter vertical clearance. Masticating juicers often are taller but there are compact models designed specifically to fit under cabinets.

### How do I clean a compact juicer efficiently?

Rinse parts immediately after use, disassemble completely, use specialized brushes for mesh filters, and choose models with dishwasher-safe components if possible. Some juicers have self-cleaning features.

### Can I put my compact juicer under a cabinet when it’s plugged in?

It’s generally best to allow some clearance for heat dissipation and avoid blocking the power cord or ventilation. Using a juicer just below a cabinet is fine if there’s enough space; consider the model’s ventilation needs stated by the manufacturer.

### Do compact juicers sacrifice juice quality?

Not necessarily. While smaller centrifugal juicers might produce slightly more foam and oxidation, many compact masticating juicers maintain excellent juice quality despite smaller footprints.
